Parental leave process - EMEA

Overview - ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave

The purpose of this article is to provide an overview of some of the key aspects of the ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave.

 

Eligibility 

The ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave applies to all regular ServiceNow employees who are actively employed and have not given or received notice of termination and are welcoming a child into their home, either through birth, adoption, foster placement, or surrogacy while employed at ServiceNow. 

Birthing Parent: The parent who is going to give or has given birth to the child. 

  • Birthing parents are entitled to up to 20 weeks of ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave
  • Birthing Parents must commence their ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave when they commence the corresponding statutory leave (e.g. Maternity leave)
  • The leave will run concurrently with and is inclusive of any statutory leave and pay/benefits to which the Birthing Parent may be entitled under local law 

Non-Birthing Parent: The parent who has not given birth to the child.

  • Non-Birthing Parents are entitled to up to 12 weeks of ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave
  • Non-Birthing Parents must commence their ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave when they commence the corresponding statutory leave (e.g. paternity/adoption leave)
  • The leave will run concurrently with and is inclusive of any statutory leave and pay/benefits to which the Birthing Parent may be entitled under local law. ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave should be taken as one continuous period of leave. However, if for business reasons this is not possible, with the prior written consent of your manager and Global People Business partner, any ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave which extends beyond the corresponding statutory leave may be split into one week increments. 

ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave must be taken within one (1) year of the birth or placement of the child. 

 

Applying for ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave 

Prior to your intent to take ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave, please complete the below steps:

  1. Review your local Parental Leave Policy and FAQs:
  • Ireland Parental Leave Policy
  • Sweden Parental Leave Policy
  • UAE Parental Leave Policy
  • UK Parental Leave Policy
  • Parental Leave FAQs
  1. Meet with your manager to discuss your intention to take parental leave and agree on the dates of your leave
  2. Get your manager's written approval of your leave dates (non-birthing parents only) - you can attach their approval email to the ticket you raise in step 5
  3. Obtain confirmation of the due date (doctor's confirmation) or date of birth of your child (birth certificate)
  4. Notify People Care via the Employee Portal no later than two months prior to the date of birth, adoption, or placement of the child. When raising the ticket please include and attach:
  • Leave dates 
  • Manager's written approval
  • Due date confirmation or birth certificate 

As the ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave Program incorporates your statutory leave and pay, you also need to follow statutory notification requirements for the relevant leave type (which might require earlier notification). 

Please note: It is not a requirement to provide your full leave plan upfront. You will be able to request your remaining leave entitlement at a later stage, so long as this complies with the process outlined above. If you would like to change any of your requested dates, please ensure to notify Global People as soon as practicably possible 

For information regarding Adoption Leave, please refer to the Global adoption reimbursement policy.
