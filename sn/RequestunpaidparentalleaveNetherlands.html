Request unpaid parental leave - Netherlands

Entitlement

You are entitled to up to 26 times your normal weekly working hours of unpaid parental leave (ouderschapsverlof) for every child up to the age of 8. This can be taken either full-time or part-time.

 

Impact on entitlement if you have taken or are taking ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave

  • For Birthing Parents (as defined in the ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave Program), 4 of the 20 weeks offered by the ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave Program are already included in the 26-week period. As such, Birthing Parents are entitled to a maximum of 22 weeks of unpaid parental leave.
  • For Non-Birthing Parents (as defined in the ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave Program), up to 11 of the 12 weeks offered by the ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave Program are already included in the 26-week period. Depending on whether you take leave within the first 6 months of the child’s life, your ServiceNow Parental leave will run concurrently with one week of statutory paternity leave and up to 5 weeks of the statutory extended partner leave. Based on this, the remaining unpaid parental leave will equal between 15 and 20 weeks total.

 

In the case of part-time unpaid parental leave, the entitlement would be adjusted to the duration that would equate to the 22 full weeks for Birthing Parents and 15 full weeks for Non-Birthing Parents. For example, for a Birthing Parent, 22 weeks would equate to 110 days or 220 half days. 

 

Please note: Once agreed to and registered, your Parental Leave must not be corrected or cancelled (even due to sickness or vacation).

 

Requesting Unpaid Parental Leave

If you would like to initiate a request for unpaid parental leave, please ensure to first discuss your intention with your manager as far in advance as possible.  Once agreed with your manager, you will need to raise a request for Global People with your manager’s approval attached, supporting evidence attached, and the schedule for the leave, at least 2 months before the first day of the requested leave. The Global People team will review your request and, if approved, you will receive a confirmation letter to confirm your leave schedule.

 

Please note: If you are requesting part-time unpaid parental leave the maximum you can request at one time is over a period of 12 months (52 weeks). If you would like to request that your part-time unpaid parental leave be extended beyond 12 months, please ensure this is first discussed with your manager and follow the process and timing above.

 

Requesting Continuation of Unpaid Parental Leave from Previous Employer

If you have taken unpaid parental leave with a previous employer in respect of the same child and you wish to take additional leave, please follow the steps outlined above. Additionally, when raising a request to Global People, kindly ensure to attach documentation from your previous employer, displaying evidence of the unpaid parental leave used and a copy of your child’s birth certificate.
