Overview - ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave

The purpose of this article is to provide an overview of some of the key aspects of the ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave.

Eligibility

The ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave applies to all regular employees who are actively employed and have not given or received notice of termination and are welcoming a child into their home, either through birth, adoption, foster placement, or surrogacy while employed at ServiceNow.

 

Birthing Parent: The parent who is going to give or has given birth to the child.

  • Birthing Parents are entitled to up to 20 weeks (includes weekends and public holidays)
  • Birthing Parents must commence their leave when they commence the corresponding statutory leave (e.g. Maternity leave)
  • The leave will run concurrently with and is inclusive of any statutory leave and pay/benefits to which the Birthing Parent may be entitled to under local law 

Non-Birthing Parent: The parent who has not given birth to the child, also includes adoption, foster placement, and delivery through surrogacy.

  • Non-Birthing Parents are entitled to up to 12 weeks (includes weekends and public holidays)
  • Non-Birthing Parents must commence their leave when they commence the corresponding statutory leave (e.g. paternity/adoption leave)
  • The leave will run concurrently with and is inclusive of any statutory leave and pay/benefits to which the Non-Birthing Parent may be entitled under local law 
  • Should be taken as one continuous period of leave. However, if for business reasons this is not possible, with the prior written consent of your manager and Global People Business partner, any leave which extends beyond the corresponding statutory leave can be taken in one week increments. 

ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave must be taken within one (1) year of the birth or placement of the child. 

Answers to questions such as benefit coverage and bonus proration can be found in the FAQs.

 

Applying for ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave 

You are required to inform your manager of your intent to take leave as soon as practically possible, then notify Global People by raising a request no later than two months prior to date of birth, adoption or placement of your child. You’ll need to tell us your planned dates of the leave.

Since the ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave incorporates your statutory leave and pay, you also need to follow statutory notification requirements for the relevant leave type (which might require earlier notification). 

ServiceNow may request that you provide evidence to support your leave. 

To request ServiceNow Paid Parental Leave or if you have any questions, please raise a request on the Employee Portal.

Please note: It is not a requirement to provide your full leave plan upfront. You will be able to request your remaining leave entitlement at a later stage, so long as this complies with the process outlined above. If you would like to change any of your requested dates or extend parental leaves further, please ensure to notify Global People as soon as possible by raising a request.
